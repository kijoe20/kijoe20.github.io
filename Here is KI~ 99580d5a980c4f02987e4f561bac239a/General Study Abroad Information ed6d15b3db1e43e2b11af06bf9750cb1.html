General Study Abroad Information

Check emails regularly to ensure no important information is missed. It's also recommended to save all emails in one folder for ease of review.

  1. Visa Procedure
  • I hold an HKSAR Passport.
  • Pre-Arrival: Student‚Äôs Pass (STP) application procedures would be done via the GEM Trailblazer Exchange/Study Abroad Portal. Applications must be submitted at least one month before start of semester.
  • Attend Offsite Formalities Exercise for Student‚Äôs Pass at NTU (preferred, appointment is needed) or Make e-appointment to complete the Student‚Äôs Pass formalities at the ICA building
  • Required documents: eForm16 (SOLAR website), In-Principal Approval (IPA) letter, Photos, Valid Passport, NTU Letter of Enrolment
  • Fee: 30 SGD (Processing Fee) + 60 SGD (Student Pass Issuance Fee)

  1. Orientation Activities
  • Before departure, online Q&A sessions are available for us to ask questions.
  • Before the semester starts, there will be an orientation talk. The PowerPoint presentation used during the talk will be sent out in a later email.
  • Other orientation activities are available for exchange students, but spaces are limited. Be sure to check your emails regularly if you'd like to join.

  1. International Services & Activities
  • The GEM Club would organise some activities for exchange students to join during the semester. Since slots are limited, be sure to check your emails frequently and sign up quickly if you would like to join these events
  • There is a GEM Club Buddy Program for exchange students to join. The program is aimed at providing a chance for us to get to know local students or other exchange students. The actual activities depend on us and our buddies. I personally love this buddy program, as I have made some very good friends through it!
  • There is no pick-up services provides by NTU ( I would mention the transportation in 11)

  1. Accommodations
  • Undergraduate housing in NTU
  • Dorms provide various options for students: single or double rooms with or without air conditioning. The cost of the hall depends on the room type. Unfortunately, there is no guarantee of which type of room you will be assigned.
  • Hall assignments are done randomly. I was assigned to Hall 8 (Double, Air-Conditioned) for the period of 4 August to 9 December 2022. My hall fee is 1389.65 SGD (7770.99 HKD). My roommate is a local Singaporean.
  • Application Procedure: Through the GEM Trailblazer Exchange/Study Abroad Portal
  • The hall payment will be made after your arrival in Singapore. NTU will send an email about the hall payment near the end of October. Please note that there may be additional charges if you are late.
  • Early check-in to the hall is not permitted. Hall movement request have to be made on the One Stop Portal (NTU online services) at least one month before the check-out date. Submitting requests late may result in not receiving a refund.
  • No meal-plans available in NTU

  1. Course Registration
  • Course Registration is done on the GEM Trailblazer Exchange/Study Abroad Portal and NTU STARS
  • Exchange Students would have to first submit a maximum of 10 courses for approval before the start of the semester through the portal.
  • Then we can register the approved courses on the STARS system when enrollment period starts
  • We can still make request for more approved course during the enrollment and add-drop period.

  1. Teaching & Assessment Methods
  • Depending on the courses, it is suggested that you check the course information in advance before enrolling.
  • NTU uses the Blackboard system (NTU LEARN), which is a similar platform to Canvas.

  1. Finance & Banking
  • Currency: 1 SGD ‚âà 6HKD (1 SGD = 5.84111 HKD Feb 24, 2023)
  • It is good to exchange some SGD in HK and bring them with you to Singapore so that you have some SGD cash to used upon arrivial
  • The student cards have NETS FlashPay, it can be used for cashless payment inside NTU, transportation and very few stores outside. It is similar to Octopus card in HK. The card can be topped up at MRT stations and convenience stores. (convenience store has additional charge for top-up)
  • I personally recommend opening a bank account after arriving in Singapore. I applied for a DBS account online, and it took about one week to process my application. Since I was staying in a hall, I was able to request proof of residence online through the NTU One Stop Portal and pick it up at the hall admin office.
  • After applying for a DBS bank account, you can use the debit card, Paylah!, and other cashless payment methods. I regret not opening a bank account earlier during my exchange period, as it is much more convenient and allows you to keep your money safe in the bank. With a bank account, you can make cashless payments almost anywhere in Singapore, and easily transfer money to your friends (PAYNOW). You can also do online shopping and use food delivery service!
  • We can also transfer money from the hk with the local account. I used Wise for transferring money from my hk bank account to singapore as it has lower transfer fee than bank transfer and ATM.

  1. Climate
  • Singapore has a tropical rainforest climate, with warm and humid weather throughout the year. The average temperature ranges from 24 to 31 degrees Celsius.
  • The climate in Singapore is similar to that of Hong Kong. Singapore have more rainfall than Hong Kong.

  1. Health & Safety
  • I recommend bring some medicine your own. There is a clinic Fullerton Health in NTU and you may see a doctor there.
  • Singapore is a safe country with low crime rate.

  1. Food
  • There many canteens in NTU campus which provide great variety of food. I also recommend hawker center nearby if you are tired of campus food.
  • There is a pantry in the hall which allow students to cook (hot and cold drinking water are available in the pantry)
  • Cates App is a app for canteen food delivery (can use paylah! for online payment)
  • I recommend Holland Village and Tanjong Pagar if you are seraching for some nice restaurants.

  1. Transportation
  • Shuttle bus service is available inside the campus.
  • Transportation in Singapore is efficient and convenient. The Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) system is one of the way to travel around the city, and there is station located near NTU. There are also buses available for transportation around the campus and city.
  • The student card has NETS FlashPay, which can be used for transportation and can be topped up at MRT stations and convenience stores. It is recommended to use public transportation as it is cost-effective
  • Grab and Gojek are popular ride-hailing and food delivery services in Singapore. They are efficient and convenient way to get around Singapore, especially for those who prefer not to use public transportation. They can be easily accessed through their mobile apps, which is available for download on iOS and Android devices.
  • To find the best price for your airline ticket, you can compare prices on Google Flights and Skyscanner.
